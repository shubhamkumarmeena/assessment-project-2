Classification of Yoga

  1. Classification based on mind and body.
    • Rajayoga: Emphasizes the most on exercise
    • Hathayoga: Emphasizes the most on exercise of physical body.
  2. Classification based on way of spiritual life.
    • Gyanyoga: It is a way of knowledge.
    • Karmayoga: It is a way of work.
    • Bhaktiyoga: It is a way of worship.
  3. Classification based on a way of other things.
    • Swaryoga: Emphasizes the most on breathing.
    • Kriyayoga: Emphasizes the most on blending Karmayoga (Tapa), Gyanyoga (Swadhyay) and Bhaktiyoga (Ishwar Pranidhan).

Meditation

1.Meditation is often defined as thinking continuously about one object of thought.
2.We often get stuck on this definition, however, and lose the real purpose of meditation.
3.Meditation must reveal the true nature of that object upon which we are meditating.
4.Such revelation comes not as a thought, but as a feeling.
5.Therefore, meditation is a process in which we shift from thinking to feeling.
6.It is a journey from the complexity of mind to the simplicity of heart.
7.It is for this reason that most methods of meditation involve the heart.


Excercise

1.Exercise is any bodily activity that enhances or maintains physical fitness and overall health and wellness.
2.It is performed for various reasons, to aid growth and improve strength, preventing aging,
3.developing muscles and the cardiovascular system, honing athletic skills, weight loss or maintenance,
4.improving health and also for enjoyment. Many individuals choose to exercise outdoors where they can congregate in groups,
5.socialize, and enhance well-being.In terms of health benefits, the amount of recommended exercise depends upon the goal,
6.the type of exercise, and the age of the person. Even doing a small amount of exercise is healthier than doing none.
